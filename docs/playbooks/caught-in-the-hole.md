# Caught in the Hole Playbook

## Summary
A profile-led reversal or reclaim playbook where price gets trapped in a structurally poor area or gap-like hole, and the opportunity forms when the market cannot sustain the auction through that weak zone.

## Status
Draft

## Playbook family
Profile-led reversal / reclaim

## Brand
The market gets stuck in bad structure, the wrong side is trapped, and the release becomes the trade.

## Context

### Regime
- structurally weak location
- trapped positioning
- reclaim / reversal after failed move through poor structure

### Event types
- none
- post-gap sessions
- post-event structure dislocation

### Session structure
- often stronger when tied to prior-session value displacement or gap-like profile condition

### Higher timeframe conditions
- stronger when poor structure is visible from daily / weekly profile context
- stronger when prior move left value behind in a vulnerable way

### Structural references
- poor TPO structure
- gap / hole / displaced value
- weak profile acceptance
- prior balance edge

### Participation conditions
- depends heavily on who is trapped and whether the market can sustain outside the weak zone

## Playbook definition

### Preceding conditions
- market leaves poor structure behind or enters bad structure without healthy acceptance
- one side becomes vulnerable if the auction cannot continue

### Mechanics
1. market trades into or through the hole
2. continuation fails to build healthy structure
3. trapped participants are forced to react
4. reclaim / reversal move develops

### Identifier
- inability to sustain in the hole
- clear failure to auction cleanly through poor structure
- signs of entrapment or refusal by the market

### Confirmation
- reclaim of structural threshold
- acceleration away from the hole
- trapped-side response becomes obvious

### Expected progression
- move away from the hole should feel cleaner than the auction inside it
- often targets prior acceptance or the other side of displaced structure

### Target logic
- prior value area
- opposite structure edge
- trapped-side unwind objective

### Invalidation
- market accepts and builds cleanly in the poor zone
- reclaim fails and structure remains weak but accepted

### Failure modes
- calling every gap or poor profile a trapped trade
- failing to identify whether anyone is actually trapped
- confusing structural ugliness with actual asymmetry

### Execution notes
- works best when profile logic and live execution cues agree
- should not rely on profile concept alone without actual behavioral shift

## Translation

### Source markets
- ES
- profile-heavy futures markets

### Transferable to
- index ETFs
- liquid stocks with obvious gap/dislocation structure

### Constraints
- strongest where profile logic is visually meaningful
