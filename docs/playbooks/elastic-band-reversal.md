# Elastic Band Reversal Playbook

## Summary
A reversal playbook where price stretches too far from meaningful structure, positioning or participation becomes vulnerable, and the snapback creates the opportunity.

## Status
Draft

## Playbook family
Stretch / mean-reversion reversal

## Brand
The market gets overextended, tension builds, and the snapback becomes the trade.

## Context

### Regime
- stretched market
- mature trend leg
- positioning vulnerability

### Event types
- none
- post-event overreaction
- headline extension sessions

### Session structure
- can form after overnight stretch, session extension, or repeated directional pushes

### Higher timeframe conditions
- strongest when extension is into a major level, distribution edge, or statistical stretch
- stronger when context says continuation is getting expensive

### Structural references
- 2std / stretched value references
- major profile edge
- prior weekly or daily extreme
- exhaustion destination

### Participation conditions
- can happen in both high and low participation, but logic differs
- strongest when late participants are vulnerable and the next push loses quality

## Playbook definition

### Preceding conditions
- market travels far from its recent center or fair zone
- continuation increasingly requires new fuel
- push into destination produces vulnerability

### Mechanics
1. market stretches
2. late directional participation enters or gets trapped
3. extension quality deteriorates
4. reversal or sharp snapback begins

### Identifier
- obvious stretch from meaningful structure
- deteriorating quality on further extension
- signs of failure to keep pressing cleanly

### Confirmation
- rejection from destination
- reclaim of an important local level
- pace shift against the stretched move

### Expected progression
- can start with violent snapback or a steadier unwind
- if strong, move often retraces toward prior accepted area

### Target logic
- return toward mean / accepted value
- revisit prior session or profile center
- structural rebound objective

### Invalidation
- stretched market accepts and keeps building in extension direction
- reversal cannot reclaim any meaningful level
- destination remains defended by strong participation

### Failure modes
- trying to fade strong trend too early
- confusing "far" with "overextended"
- ignoring regime where extension is actually discovery, not exhaustion

### Execution notes
- best when stretch aligns with context and weak continuation quality
- often improved by pairing structure with order-flow trigger
