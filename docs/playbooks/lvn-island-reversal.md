# LVN Island Reversal Playbook

## Summary
A reversal playbook where price breaks or pushes into a low-volume pocket, forms an isolated acceptance area or "island," and then reverses once the attempted auction fails to continue.

## Status
Draft

## Playbook family
Reversal

## Brand
Price leaves the well-traded area, gets stranded in thin air, and snaps back when the auction cannot build acceptance.

## Context

### Regime
- low to medium participation
- stretched move into reference
- failed directional auction

### Event types
- none
- post-event cleanups
- lower-energy sessions after a prior move

### Session structure
- often strongest when price extends away from established value into a thin area
- can develop after overnight push or intraday extension

### Higher timeframe conditions
- approach into a meaningful higher-timeframe level helps
- stronger when prior move is already mature or vulnerable

### Structural references
- LVN pocket
- edge of value
- poor high/low or weak TPO structure
- prior weekly or daily reference

### Participation conditions
- not usually the highest-energy trend day
- often better in slower or more rotational environments

## Playbook definition

### Preceding conditions
- market extends out of established structure
- new area fails to build healthy acceptance
- auction gets thin and vulnerable

### Mechanics
1. extension into LVN or poor structure
2. temporary hold or island-like acceptance
3. failure to continue
4. reversal back through the weak area

### Identifier
- isolated trade zone with weak continuation
- personality shift or slowing into the extension
- inability to build acceptance beyond the thin zone

### Confirmation
- reversal back through the island edge
- opposing flow takes control with cleaner pace
- inability of original direction to defend the extension

### Expected progression
- move back through the weak area can be fast
- if clean, reversion can continue toward prior value or origin reference

### Target logic
- return to prior accepted value
- revisit cave/value edge
- test of prior balance reference

### Invalidation
- price holds and builds healthy trade beyond the island
- reversal cannot reclaim the island edge
- original direction reasserts with strong participation

### Failure modes
- trader calls every thin area an island
- higher timeframe trend is too strong for reversal to matter
- no meaningful reference, so the reversal has no real asymmetry

### Execution notes
- often improved by combining profile read with ladder confirmation
- access can be aggressive near stall or more conservative on reclaim
