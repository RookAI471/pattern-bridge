# Cave Fill Reclaim Playbook

## Summary
A structural playbook built around price entering, filling, or sweeping a cave/thin distribution area and then reclaiming or reversing from that zone when the attempted continuation cannot sustain.

## Status
Draft

## Playbook family
Structural reclaim / reversal

## Brand
Price goes back into the cave, does the repair, and then shows whether the fill was completion or trap.

## Context

### Regime
- structural repair
- failed continuation
- reclaim or reversal after weak auctioning through thin structure

### Event types
- none
- post-event cleanup
- post-gap repair

### Session structure
- often linked to overnight inventory, prior day structure, or multi-session references

### Higher timeframe conditions
- stronger when cave relates to weekly or prior-session imbalance
- stronger when return to cave aligns with mature move or incomplete repair logic

### Structural references
- cave top / cave bottom
- single prints
- poor TPO structure
- prior value relationship
- gap repair zones

### Participation conditions
- can appear in both slower and faster sessions
- quality depends on whether the fill is orderly, impulsive, or exhausted

## Playbook definition

### Preceding conditions
- market has left behind a structurally weak area
- later return into that area occurs
- traders must determine whether repair completes and continues or exhausts and reverses

### Mechanics
1. return into cave or thin area
2. fill / sweep / repair begins
3. market either completes and accepts, or rejects and reclaims
4. playbook forms around reclaim or failure behavior

### Identifier
- visible interaction with cave edge
- inability to sustain deeper into cave after fill attempt
- order-flow shift around repair completion zone

### Confirmation
- reclaim of cave edge or decisive response from cave boundary
- cleaner pace away from the repaired area
- inability of repair move to keep pressing through structure

### Expected progression
- if reclaim is real, move away from the cave should feel cleaner than the fill action
- targets often connect to prior accepted structure or opposite cave edge

### Target logic
- opposite edge of cave
- prior value area
- prior directional objective if reclaim becomes broader continuation

### Invalidation
- market accepts and holds inside the cave beyond expected tolerance
- reclaim cannot hold key cave boundary

### Failure modes
- trader assumes every cave touch must reverse
- structure is still being legitimately repaired and accepted
- fill completes but trader mistakes pause for rejection

### Execution notes
- often requires structural patience
- can benefit from pairing profile concept with ladder trigger
