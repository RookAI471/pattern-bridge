# User Personas

## Purpose

This document defines the first user personas for Pattern Bridge.

The goal is not perfect market segmentation.
The goal is to identify who feels the pain sharply enough to care, adopt, and give useful feedback.

## Product principle

Pattern Bridge is for traders who believe context matters.

It is not for people who only want:
- hot alerts
- dopamine scanners
- guaranteed picks
- autopilot execution

## Primary persona

# 1. The serious discretionary trader

## Description
A trader who already thinks in terms of context, structure, and execution, but does not have a strong system for memory, retrieval, and playbook refinement.

## Typical traits
- trades actively and reviews at least some of their work
- uses charts, levels, and macro context
- may use watchlists, notes, or screenshots inconsistently
- knows context matters but struggles to preserve and retrieve it well
- wants better judgment support, not a black-box bot

## Pain points
- forgets prior similar situations
- has fragmented notes and screenshots
- debriefs inconsistently
- sees patterns but cannot formalize them cleanly
- wastes time manually searching for analogs
- has weak recall of failed lookalikes

## Why they would want Pattern Bridge
- to retrieve relevant prior examples fast
- to build and refine playbooks with less manual pain
- to compare current context to past cases
- to improve premarket framing and post-session debrief

## Why they might reject it
- if it feels like scanner fluff
- if it is too much admin work
- if it acts like an overconfident AI guru

---

# 2. The developing playbook builder

## Description
A trader who knows they need more structure but does not naturally document well.

## Typical traits
- has some experience but still feels messy
- knows they repeat mistakes
- knows they should journal and review more
- often sees "something there" but cannot define it rigorously

## Pain points
- hates documentation
- gets overwhelmed by note systems
- cannot easily turn examples into reusable patterns
- lacks confidence in what actually constitutes an edge
- often confuses lookalikes for the real thing

## Why they would want Pattern Bridge
- to reduce documentation burden
- to receive candidate bridges for evaluation
- to build a cleaner review habit
- to develop their own playbook library over time

## Why they might reject it
- if the workflow feels too academic
- if it takes too long to use daily
- if it assumes they already have a mature bridge library

---

# 3. The coach / mentor / trading group lead

## Description
A coach, educator, or trading lead who wants a better framework for reviewing examples and discussing contextual fit with students or team members.

## Typical traits
- teaches process, not just entries
- reviews charts and examples with others
- wants a shared language for discussing what did and did not fit
- sees repeated student failure around context and debriefing

## Pain points
- students overfocus on setup names without context
- repetitive manual review work
- weak shared memory across examples
- hard to organize winning and failed examples coherently

## Why they would want Pattern Bridge
- to build a shared playbook language
- to organize examples and analogs
- to accelerate debrief quality
- to capture variants and false lookalikes systematically

## Why they might reject it
- if it is too individual and not collaborative
- if it cannot explain why something surfaced
- if it turns nuanced teaching into simplistic automation

---

## Secondary personas

# 4. The stock scanner power user evolving upward

## Description
A trader who already uses scanners heavily but wants more context and better analog reasoning.

## Why interesting
This user may feel the stock-wedge value quickly.

## Risk
They may pull the product toward commodity scanning unless the product remains disciplined.

---

# 5. The systematic-curious discretionary trader

## Description
A trader who likes structure and repeatability but still relies heavily on discretion and contextual reading.

## Why interesting
They may be especially drawn to bridge schemas, scoring, and consistent review loops.

## Risk
They may push too hard toward false quantification of discretionary nuance.

---

## Who is not the user

Pattern Bridge is not primarily for:
- absolute beginners who do not yet understand market structure
- pure signal chasers
- people seeking guaranteed trade calls
- people who do not believe in review, context, or discretionary judgment
- fully systematic traders who have no need for discretionary analog workflows

## Best initial persona for MVP

The best initial persona is:
**the serious discretionary trader**

Why:
- sharp pain
- strong feedback quality
- already values context
- likely to appreciate analog retrieval and debrief loops
- less likely to misuse the product as a dumb alert feed

## Best commercial wedge after MVP

Potentially:
**the stock-oriented trader who wants better candidate discovery without losing judgment**

That is where the scaling opportunity may become more obvious.

## Design implications by persona

### Serious discretionary trader needs
- clear context framing
- strong analog retrieval
- good failure-case memory
- low-friction debrief workflow

### Developing playbook builder needs
- structure without overload
- stronger guidance
- easier note-to-playbook conversion
- more educational scaffolding

### Coach / mentor needs
- explainable candidate surfacing
- shared examples
- collaborative review
- taxonomy and teaching language

## Product tone implication

The product should sound like:
- a sharp thinking partner
- a memory and review assistant
- a context coach

It should not sound like:
- a hypey guru
- a trading bot salesman
- a generic growth-hack SaaS

## Closing view

If the product is built well, the first loyal users will likely be traders who already know that context matters but are tired of trying to hold the whole pattern library in their head.
