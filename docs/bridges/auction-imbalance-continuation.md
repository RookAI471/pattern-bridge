# Auction Imbalance Continuation

## Summary
A continuation bridge built around visible auction imbalance, strong directional participation, and a retest or access point that allows continuation in the original direction.

## Status
Draft

## Family
Continuation

## Brand
The move is real, the retest proves it, and the imbalance becomes access.

## Context

### Regime
- trend
- event-driven continuation
- high-participation session

### Event types
- none
- CPI
- FOMC
- NFP
- catalyst sessions with strong directional energy

### Session structure
- can appear intraday without overnight dependency
- stronger when tied to a meaningful reference or prior directional impulse

### Higher timeframe conditions
- strong directional bias already present
- breakout or directional impulse from higher timeframe reference
- market not obviously trapped in a dead rotational session

### Structural references
- prior range high/low
- cave edge
- breakout point
- high-volume retest area
- obvious directional reference

### Participation conditions
- elevated RVOL or pace
- obvious pressure in one direction
- move has enough size to matter

## Pattern definition

### Preceding conditions
- signal bar or impulse move establishes directional intent
- directional bar stands out in pace, volume, or range
- market pulls back without fully negating the impulse

### Mechanics
1. directional impulse prints
2. imbalance is visible in the move
3. pullback/retest forms access
4. continuation confirms through renewed directional pressure

### Identifier
- strong signal bar or directional impulse
- evidence of imbalance and weak opposing response on pullback

### Confirmation
- continuation pressure returns after retest
- pace and ease of movement resume in original direction
- access does not lose the defining imbalance area cleanly

### Expected progression
- continuation should feel purposeful, not limp
- if right, the move resumes without requiring endless rescue
- extension can grind or accelerate depending on session tempo

### Target logic
- original imbalance area extension
- next major structural reference
- measured continuation zone

### Invalidation
- retest fully loses the key imbalance structure
- opposing side absorbs and holds beyond expected tolerance
- move becomes rotation rather than continuation

### Failure modes
- directional impulse looked strong but occurred in poor context
- retest is too deep and actually signals exhaustion
- trader mistakes a one-bar spike for true directional participation

### Execution notes
- often best on retest rather than first breakout chase
- can support aggressive and conservative access variants

## Translation

### Source markets
- ES
- CL

### Transferable to
- index futures
- liquid large-cap stocks
- sector leaders after catalyst-driven breaks

### Constraints
- requires enough participation to distinguish real imbalance from noise
- thin names may fake this pattern constantly
